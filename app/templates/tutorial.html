Tutorial

Learn how to use the table and map to explore species prioritization data.

The Table

Explore species data row-by-row with filtering, sorting, and custom scoring.

Searching & Filtering

Use the search box at the top of the table to filter species by name, family, or any other field. Results update instantly as you type, so you can quickly zero in on the species you're interested in.

Sorting Columns

Click any column header to sort the table. The first click sorts in ascending order, and clicking again reverses the direction. This helps you quickly find the highest-priority species or group by threat category.

Adjusting Scoring Weights

Open the sliders panel to customize how each IUCN threat level and DNA sampling status contributes to the priority score. Want to prioritize critically endangered species over data deficient ones? Drag the sliders. Your adjustments recalculate the entire table in real-time, so you can explore different strategies instantly.

{# #}

Sharing Your Configuration

When you've configured weights that you want to share with colleagues or save for later, just copy the URL from your browser. The entire state—search filters, sort order, and all slider values—is encoded in the link. Anyone who opens it will see your exact setup.

Pagination

Navigate between pages using the arrow buttons at the bottom, or use your keyboard (← and → keys) for faster navigation. Each page displays 10 species, so you can steadily work through the full dataset without overwhelming your screen.

#

The Map

Visualize species distribution and priority across Denmark's hexagonal grid.

Zooming & Panning

Scroll your mouse wheel to zoom in and out. Drag the map to pan across Denmark. The map intelligently switches between coarse hexagon resolution (when zoomed far out) and fine resolution (when zoomed in) to balance detail and performance.

Color Intensity

Each hexagon's color represents the priority score for species in that area. Dark colors indicate lower priority, while bright yellow-green indicates higher priority. The color scale dynamically adjusts to your current slider weights, so you can immediately see how your scoring changes the geographic distribution of priorities.

{# #}

Hovering & Details

Hover your mouse over any hexagon to see a detailed tooltip. It shows threat counts by category, missing DNA levels at each taxonomic rank, and the raw score breakdown. This lets you understand exactly why a region has its current priority ranking.

Adjusting Weights in Real-Time

Open the sliders panel while viewing the map and drag any weight slider. Watch the hexagon colors shift instantly across the entire map as the scoring function recalculates. This is a powerful way to explore how different prioritization strategies would affect your sampling strategy geographically.

Fullscreen Mode

Click the fullscreen icon in the top-right corner of the map to expand it to your entire screen. This maximizes your workspace and makes it easier to spot patterns and hotspots across the hexagonal grid.

Ready to explore? Start with the table or jump to the map.
